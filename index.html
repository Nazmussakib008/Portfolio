Research Experience

Site Suitability Modelling of Asian Sea Bass (Lates calcarifer) along the Southeastern Coast of Bangladesh.

Undergraduate Research Project | 2023

The primary objective of this study is to identify suitable habitat zones for the Asian sea bass (Lates calcarifer) along the southeastern coast of Bangladesh.We applied a Generalized Additive Model (GAM) incorporating presence–absence data, in situ measurements of physical environmental parameters, satellite-derived variables, and citizen science observations.The results generated a spatial distribution map highlighting the occurrence and potential habitat suitability of Asian sea bass in the study area.Model performance indicated a moderate predictive capability, with an Area Under the Curve (AUC) value of 0.6519.The predicted habitat suitability ranged from 20% to 70%, suggesting variable levels of occurrence across the region.This study provides valuable insights into the spatial distribution of Lates calcarifer and can support informed decision-making for resource management, commercial fisheries, and aquaculture development initiatives.

Supervisors: MD Shajjadur Rahman, Assistant Professor Department of Oceanography, Shahjalal University of Science and Technology, Sylhet-3114, Bangladesh.

Research Experience

Assessing the Effects of Environmental Drivers on the Growth of Asian Sea Bass(Lates calcarifer) and Mapping Potential Growth Zones in the Bakkhali–Maheshkhali Estuary

Graduate Research Project | 2025

This study examined the effects of environmental factors on the Growth of Asian sea bass (Lates calcarifer) in the Moheshkhali-Bakkhali estuary using a Generalized Additive Model. Salinity (15–25 ppt) and temperature (25.5–28.5 °C) were the main drivers of growth, while alkalinity (111–129 mg/L), pH (7.2–7.8), and dissolved oxygen (5.5–7.1 mg/L) also positively influenced weight gain.The estuary, particularly during summer, was identified as highly favorable for growth, highlighting its potential for sustainable aquaculture aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals.

Supervisor: Dr. Md. Ahsanul Islam,Associate Professor,Department of Oceanography, Shahjalal University Of Science and Technology sylhet
